ASRS REPORT — ACN 1987654

NARRATIVE:
During approach to runway 28R at SFO, we were conducting an ILS approach in IMC conditions with visibility reported at 1/2 mile. At approximately 800 feet AGL, the First Officer who was the pilot flying called "500 feet, not stabilized" as our airspeed was VREF+25 and we were one dot above the glideslope. I called for a go-around but the FO hesitated momentarily before initiating the go-around. 

We executed the missed approach procedure and were vectored for a second approach. On the second attempt, we maintained stabilized approach criteria and landed without incident.

Contributing factors include: gusty wind conditions (reported 15G25), the FO's limited experience with SFO approaches in low visibility, and a late configuration change requested by ATC (we were told to maintain 180 knots to the outer marker).

The ATC speed restriction delayed our ability to configure the aircraft in time for a normal descent profile. I recommend that approach control be made aware of the impact late speed restrictions have on approach stability.

FLIGHT PHASE: Approach
AIRCRAFT: B737-800
WEATHER: IMC, visibility 1/2SM, winds 250/15G25
