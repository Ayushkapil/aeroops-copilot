ASRS REPORT — ACN 2034521

NARRATIVE:
While taxiing for departure at ATL on taxiway Bravo, we were cleared to cross runway 27L. As we began to cross, we noticed a regional jet on short final for 27L approximately 1 mile out. We immediately stopped on the runway and advised tower. Tower then issued a go-around to the arriving aircraft.

Investigation revealed that the local controller had cleared us to cross while forgetting about the arriving traffic. The ground controller had handed us off to tower frequency but there was a miscommunication about our position. We were on taxiway B3 which intersects 27L.

The runway incursion was classified as category B — significant potential for collision. No evasive action was required by the arriving aircraft as the go-around was initiated in time, but the separation was reduced to approximately 0.8 miles.

I recommend enhanced coordination procedures between ground and local controllers, particularly during peak traffic periods at ATL.

FLIGHT PHASE: Taxi
AIRCRAFT: A320
WEATHER: VMC, clear skies
