INSTRUMENT APPROACH PROCEDURES — STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE

Section 1.0 — Scope
This procedure covers the conduct of instrument approaches in accordance with FAR 91.175, AIM Chapter 5, and company policy.

Section 2.0 — Approach Types and Minimums
2.1 Precision Approaches (PA): ILS, GLS — vertical and lateral guidance. Decision Altitude (DA).
2.2 Approaches with Vertical Guidance (APV): LPV, LNAV/VNAV — advisory vertical guidance. Decision Altitude (DA).
2.3 Non-Precision Approaches (NPA): VOR, NDB, LNAV, LOC — lateral guidance only. Minimum Descent Altitude (MDA).

Section 3.0 — Approach Briefing
The approach briefing SHALL include:
1. Type of approach and runway
2. Navigation frequencies and courses
3. Decision altitude/MDA and visibility requirements
4. Missed approach procedure and altitude
5. Airport diagram and taxi plan
6. NOTAMs affecting the approach
7. Weather at destination and alternate
8. Go-around procedure including thrust setting and initial altitude

Section 4.0 — Approach Execution
4.1 ILS Approach:
- Intercept localizer from assigned heading
- Maintain localizer within 1 dot
- Capture glideslope from below
- Maintain glideslope within 1 dot
- Cross FAF at published altitude
- Begin timing if required

4.2 RNAV (GPS) Approach:
- Verify GPS RAIM availability
- Load and activate approach in FMS
- Verify waypoint sequence matches published procedure
- Monitor CDI scaling changes (terminal to approach mode)
- Cross waypoints at published altitudes

Section 5.0 — Decision Point Actions
5.1 At DA/DH (Precision):
- If runway environment is in sight: continue to land
- If runway environment is NOT in sight: execute missed approach immediately
- Runway environment includes: approach lights, threshold, runway markings, VASI/PAPI

5.2 At MDA (Non-Precision):
- Level off at MDA
- Continue to MAP at approach speed
- If runway environment is in sight before MAP: descend for landing
- If runway environment is NOT in sight at MAP: execute missed approach

WARNING: Do NOT descend below DA/DH or MDA without the required visual references.

Section 6.0 — Missed Approach
1. Advance thrust to TOGA or go-around power
2. Pitch up to initial climb attitude
3. Retract flaps as directed by go-around procedure
4. Follow published missed approach procedure
5. Advise ATC: "Going around"
6. Clean up aircraft and brief for next approach or diversion

Section 7.0 — CAT II and CAT III Operations
CAT II/III approaches require:
- Authorized crew training and currency
- Qualified aircraft and equipment (dual autopilot, HUD)
- Appropriate airport facilities (CAT II/III lighting, RVR reporting)
- DA as low as 100 feet (CAT II) or 0 feet (CAT IIIC)
- Autoland capability for CAT IIIB/C
NOTE: CAT III operations require both crew members to be qualified and current.

Section 8.0 — Circling Approaches
1. Maintain circling MDA until in position to land
2. Keep the runway in sight at all times during circling
3. If runway is lost during circling: immediately execute missed approach — turn toward the landing runway
4. Circling radius limitations apply based on aircraft category
CAUTION: Circling approaches at night or in low visibility significantly increase risk. Use extreme caution.
