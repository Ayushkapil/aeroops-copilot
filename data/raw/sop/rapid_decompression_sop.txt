RAPID DECOMPRESSION AND EMERGENCY DESCENT — STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE

Section 1.0 — Scope
This procedure covers flight crew response to cabin depressurization events including rapid and explosive decompression, per FAR 91.211 and AC 120-80A.

Section 2.0 — Recognition
Signs of rapid decompression:
- Loud bang or rushing air sound
- Fog/mist in cabin due to rapid temperature/pressure change
- Cabin altitude warning horn
- Rapid rise in cabin altitude gauge
- Ear pain, difficulty breathing
- Unsecured items blown toward breach
- Oxygen masks deploy automatically at 14,000 feet cabin altitude

Section 3.0 — Immediate Actions (Memory Items)
Upon recognition of rapid decompression:
1. DON OXYGEN MASKS — set to 100% / EMERGENCY / PRESSURE
2. Establish crew communication via interphone
3. Pilot Flying: EMERGENCY DESCENT — disconnect autopilot
4. Simultaneously:
   a. Thrust levers — IDLE
   b. Speed brakes — FLIGHT DETENT (extend)
   c. Bank — 45 to 90 degrees (for rapid altitude loss and terrain clearance)
   d. Begin descent at maximum operating speed (VMO/MMO)
5. Squawk 7700
6. Declare MAYDAY: "MAYDAY, MAYDAY, MAYDAY. [Callsign] rapid decompression. Emergency descent. Leaving [altitude]"
7. Turn toward nearest suitable airport if known, or descend in place

WARNING: Time of useful consciousness at 40,000 feet is 15-20 seconds. GET YOUR MASK ON FIRST.

Section 4.0 — Descent Profile
- Target altitude: 10,000 feet MSL (or MEA if higher due to terrain)
- Do NOT exceed VMO/MMO during descent
- Level off at 10,000 feet and reduce to 250 knots
- Maintain 10,000 feet or lower until landing
- If terrain requires higher altitude, use minimum safe altitude with supplemental oxygen

Section 5.0 — During Descent
1. Pilot Monitoring: advise ATC of emergency and intentions
2. Check passenger oxygen status via cabin crew
3. Assess structural damage if any
4. Plan diversion to nearest suitable airport with:
   - Long runway (structural integrity may be compromised)
   - Emergency services available
   - Weather suitable for approach

Section 6.0 — Post-Emergency
1. Maintain oxygen until cabin altitude is confirmed below 10,000 feet
2. Brief passengers on situation
3. Request priority handling from ATC
4. Land as soon as practical — do NOT continue to destination
5. Aircraft requires structural inspection before next flight
6. File incident report and notify maintenance

Section 7.0 — Supplemental Oxygen Duration
- Flight crew oxygen: typically 2+ hours at full flow
- Passenger chemical generators: approximately 12-22 minutes
- Portable oxygen bottles: verify before each flight
CAUTION: Passenger oxygen duration is limited. Descend to breathable altitude as rapidly as possible.
