SMOKE AND FUMES EMERGENCY PROCEDURES — STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE

Section 1.0 — Scope
This procedure covers flight crew response to smoke, fumes, or unusual odors detected in the cockpit or cabin, in accordance with AC 120-80A.

Section 2.0 — Immediate Actions (Memory Items)
Upon detection of smoke or fumes:
1. Don oxygen masks — set to 100% / EMERGENCY
2. Establish crew communication via interphone
3. Declare MAYDAY to ATC
4. Begin descent and divert to nearest suitable airport

Section 3.0 — Smoke Source Identification
After immediate actions are complete:
- If smoke source is identified: Execute the appropriate checklist for that system
- If smoke source is NOT identified: Execute the Smoke/Fumes of Unknown Origin checklist
- Consider electrical load shedding: Turn off non-essential electrical systems systematically

Section 4.0 — Electrical Load Shedding
If smoke is suspected to be electrical in origin:
1. Turn off galley power
2. Turn off passenger entertainment systems
3. Turn off non-essential bus loads
4. Monitor: if smoke stops after turning off a specific system, leave it off
5. Do NOT re-energize any system that was associated with smoke

Section 5.0 — Cabin Crew Coordination
- Brief cabin crew on situation via interphone
- Cabin crew to locate smoke source if safe to do so
- Prepare cabin for emergency landing
- If fire is visible: use appropriate extinguisher (Halon for electrical, water for non-electrical)

Section 6.0 — Landing Priority
Smoke and fumes events are treated as emergencies. Request priority handling from ATC:
- Shortest available approach to nearest suitable runway
- Emergency services standing by
- Consider landing overweight if necessary — structural inspection required post-landing

Section 7.0 — Post-Landing
- Evacuate aircraft if smoke continues after landing
- Do NOT shut down engines until evacuation is complete (if smoke is in cabin area only)
- Contact maintenance and file an Irregular Operations Report
