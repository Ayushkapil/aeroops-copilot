STABILIZED APPROACH CRITERIA — STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE

Section 1.0 — Purpose
This document defines the criteria for a stabilized approach in accordance with FAA Advisory Circular AC 120-71A and ICAO best practices.

Section 2.0 — Stabilized Approach Definition
An approach is considered stabilized when all of the following criteria are met before reaching 1,000 feet AGL in IMC or 500 feet AGL in VMC:

Section 2.1 — Airspeed Criteria
The aircraft must be at the correct approach speed (VREF + wind correction), not exceeding VREF + 20 knots and not below VREF. Speed deviations greater than 10 knots require a go-around.

Section 2.2 — Configuration
The aircraft must be in the planned landing configuration with landing gear down and locked, flaps set to the landing position, and all checklists completed.

Section 2.3 — Descent Rate
The descent rate shall not exceed 1,000 feet per minute. If the descent rate exceeds 1,500 FPM at any point below 1,000 feet AGL, a go-around is mandatory.

Section 2.4 — Lateral Deviation
The aircraft must be on the correct flight path with no more than one dot lateral deviation on the localizer or LNAV path.

Section 2.5 — Vertical Deviation
The aircraft must be on the correct glide path with no more than one dot vertical deviation on the glideslope or VNAV path.

Section 3.0 — Go-Around Criteria
A go-around MUST be executed if any of the following occur:
- The approach is not stabilized by the minimum stabilization altitude
- Airspeed deviation exceeds VREF + 20 knots or falls below VREF - 5 knots
- Descent rate exceeds 1,500 FPM below 1,000 feet AGL
- Excessive lateral deviation from the runway centerline
- Any doubt about the safety of the approach
- ATC instruction to go around
- Runway not clear or not visible at minimums

Section 4.0 — Crew Responsibilities
The Pilot Flying (PF) is responsible for maintaining the stabilized approach parameters. The Pilot Monitoring (PM) is responsible for calling out deviations and initiating go-around calls when criteria are not met.

Section 5.0 — Documentation
All unstabilized approaches and go-arounds must be documented in the flight report and reported through the Safety Management System (SMS).
