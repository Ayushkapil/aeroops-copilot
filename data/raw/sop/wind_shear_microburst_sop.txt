WIND SHEAR AND MICROBURST PROCEDURES — STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE

Section 1.0 — Scope
This procedure covers detection, avoidance, and escape procedures for wind shear and microbursts during takeoff, approach, and landing, per FAA AC 00-54.

Section 2.0 — Definitions
2.1 Wind Shear: A sudden change in wind speed and/or direction over a short distance, causing rapid changes in airspeed and flight path.
2.2 Microburst: An intense, localized column of sinking air producing damaging divergent winds at the surface. Duration: 5-15 minutes. Diameter: 1-3 miles.
2.3 LLWAS: Low Level Wind Shear Alert System — airport-based detection.
2.4 PIREP: Pilot Reports of wind shear encounters.

Section 3.0 — Pre-Flight Planning
1. Review SIGMETS, AIRMETS, and PIREPs for wind shear activity
2. Check Terminal Aerodrome Forecast (TAF) for convective activity
3. Brief wind shear escape maneuver during departure/arrival briefing
4. Consider alternate airports if severe wind shear is forecast

Section 4.0 — Indicators of Wind Shear
- Sudden airspeed changes of 15 knots or more
- Sudden altitude deviations
- Unusual pitch attitude required to maintain glide path
- GPWS wind shear warning activation
- Heavy rain followed by ring of dust on the surface (visual indicator of microburst)
- Virga (precipitation not reaching the ground)

Section 5.0 — Wind Shear Escape Maneuver — Takeoff
If wind shear is encountered during takeoff:
1. Advance thrust levers to maximum takeoff thrust (TOGA)
2. Rotate to initial pitch attitude of 15 degrees nose up
3. Follow flight director if wind shear guidance is available
4. Do NOT retract landing gear until positive rate is confirmed AND shear is exited
5. Do NOT change flap configuration until clear of shear
6. Do NOT attempt to maintain altitude at the expense of airspeed
NOTE: Trading altitude for airspeed is acceptable — stall is the primary threat.

Section 6.0 — Wind Shear Escape Maneuver — Approach
If wind shear is encountered on approach:
1. Advance thrust to TOGA
2. Rotate to 15 degrees nose up
3. If stick shaker activates, do NOT exceed stick shaker pitch attitude
4. Follow flight director wind shear guidance
5. Do NOT retract flaps or gear during the escape
6. Climb to safe altitude before reconfiguring

Section 7.0 — Avoidance
- Delay takeoff/landing if wind shear is reported or detected
- Increase approach speed by half the gust factor plus the full headwind loss
- Maintain maximum thrust until clear of the shear zone
- If microburst is reported within 3 miles of intended flight path, DELAY operations
CAUTION: Do NOT attempt to fly through a known microburst. The risk is unacceptable.

Section 8.0 — Reporting
All wind shear encounters must be reported to ATC immediately via PIREP. Include: location, altitude, airspeed change, and flight conditions.
